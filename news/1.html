Leo Turini, a veteran of Italian motorsport journalism, could not stay away from the story of the court case “Felipe Massa vs. FIA, FOM, and Bernie Ecclestone”, since in the fall of 2008 he was present both at the Singapore Grand Prix and at the Brazilian season finale…

I’m writing about this because I owe it to Felipe Massa.

Of course, what happened back then in Singapore is a matter of days long gone. Everyone who was in kindergarten in 2008 has already grown up, become young adults in their twenties, and has every right not to know what I’m about to talk about.

But for old eccentrics like me, it’s quite typical to mistakenly believe that the current generation should be aware of things about which they actually have no idea.

Nevertheless, I’ll continue. In 2008, I was at the Singapore Grand Prix. That same year, I was also in Interlagos. And now, allow me to ask: how much is it worth to lose a World Championship — especially when it’s done so impressively, almost theatrically?

A little more than 80 million dollars — that’s the amount Massa intends to recover from those responsible for that incredible finale to the 2008 championship, and the trial in the Royal Court of London is already underway…
