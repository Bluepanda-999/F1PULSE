Senior management representatives of the new Cadillac F1 team have repeatedly assured that the work on building their first Formula 1 car is progressing on schedule — and this has now been confirmed by Pat Symonds, who serves as the team’s Executive Engineering Consultant for the project.

“We are completely on schedule,” said 72-year-old Symonds in an interview with The Athletic. “The date by which our first chassis must be ready for homologation was set many months ago — and the car will be completed by that day.

The situation is somewhat intimidating, but everyone at Cadillac is showing incredible determination. Until March 7 of this year, we didn’t even have official approval to enter the championship, and by then there were only 364 days left before the first practice session in Australia. Building a full team in that time is almost impossible.”

Cadillac, however, began its preparations long before the FIA officially approved the 11th team’s entry. For example, wind tunnel testing of the prototype had already started in 2024.
