Yesterday, on the final day of official Formula E testing in Valencia, only women took the wheel — every team in the world championship provided its electric car to one of the top female drivers. This year marked the second time such tests were held, and, as previously reported, their duration was doubled compared to last year.

In both sessions, American driver Chloe Chambers, a member of the Red Bull Junior Team, dominated the field. Chambers currently races for Campos Racing in the F1 Academy and sits fourth in the standings with one round remaining.

Results of the Women’s Test

Several experienced Formula E race drivers supported and coached the female participants, offering advice and helping them avoid mistakes. Chambers mentioned this in her post-test comments:

“We worked really well together with the team, both on the simulator and during the test in Valencia. It was fantastic to have Nyck de Vries helping me — thanks to his valuable advice, I was able to be among the fastest right away, which is obviously a great sign.

We ran quite a few short stints to set competitive lap times and also practiced race starts — it was really exciting and fun. I was amazed by how the all-wheel-drive electric car responds to throttle input!”
